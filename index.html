Emma Timmins-Schiffman

Research Scientist, Nunn Lab · University of Washington

Emma Timmins-Schiffman is a Research Scientist in the Nunn Lab at the University of Washington, studying the molecular mechanisms underlying interactions between marine populations and their environment. Her work spans population genetics, metagenomics, and transcriptomics, with a current focus on proteomics applied to marine organisms ranging from bacteria to bivalves. She received her MS from Stanford University in Biological Sciences and her PhD from the University of Washington in Aquatic and Fishery Sciences.
